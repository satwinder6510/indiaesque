## CATEGORY 1: General Delhi Travel (Hub + PAA)

### Hub Page

### PAA Pages
☐ **Is 2 days enough for Delhi?**
→ Yes for highlights, no for depth. Provide 2-day itinerary. Link to "how to spend 3 days" page.

☐ **How to spend 3 days in Delhi?**
→ Day-by-day itinerary. Day 1: Old Delhi. Day 2: New Delhi monuments. Day 3: South Delhi + experiences.

☐ **How to spend 1 day in Delhi?**
→ Tight itinerary. Morning: Old Delhi (Red Fort, Chandni Chowk, Jama Masjid). Afternoon: Humayun's Tomb, Lodhi Gardens. Evening: Connaught Place.

☐ **How many days do you need in Delhi?**
→ 2-3 days minimum. 4-5 to go deeper. Explain what you can cover in each timeframe.

☐ **What is Delhi best known for?**
→ History (7 ancient cities), street food, Mughal architecture, markets, political capital.

☐ **What is the most visited place in Delhi?**
→ Red Fort, Qutub Minar, India Gate. Include visitor numbers if available.

☐ **Is Delhi worth visiting?**
→ Yes, but set expectations. Not a beach holiday. Chaos is the point. What makes it unique.

☐ **What is the best time to visit Delhi?**
→ October-March (cool season). Avoid April-June (extreme heat 45°C+). Monsoon July-Sept (humid but fewer crowds).

☐ **What is the best month to visit Delhi?**
→ November or February. November: post-monsoon freshness, Diwali. February: pleasant, Delhi Lit Fest.

☐ **Is Delhi safe for tourists?**
→ Generally yes. Petty theft/scams are main risk. Metro is safe. Avoid isolated areas at night. Use Uber/Ola not street taxis.

☐ **Is Delhi safe for solo female travellers?**
→ Dress modestly, use women's metro carriage, Uber/Ola at night, stick to populated areas. Thousands do it safely.

☐ **Is Delhi safe at night?**
→ Parts of it. South Delhi, Connaught Place, Khan Market area are fine. Avoid Paharganj, Old Delhi lanes after dark.

☐ **What should I avoid in Delhi?**
→ Street taxis (use Uber), tap water, walking alone at night in quiet areas, aggressive touts near Red Fort/Jama Masjid.

☐ **Is Delhi expensive?**
→ Cheap by Western standards. Budget ₹2,000-3,000/day ($25-35). Mid-range ₹5,000-10,000/day. Luxury ₹15,000+.

☐ **How much money do I need per day in Delhi?**
→ Break down: accommodation, food, transport, entry fees. Budget/mid/luxury tiers.

☐ **Where should I stay in Delhi as a tourist?**
→ Connaught Place (central, walkable), Karol Bagh (budget), South Delhi (upscale, safe), Paharganj (backpacker but rough).

☐ **How do I get from Delhi airport to the city?**
→ Airport Express Metro (fastest, ₹60, 20 mins to New Delhi station), Uber/Ola (₹300-500), prepaid taxi (₹400-600).

☐ **Can you drink tap water in Delhi?**
→ No. Bottled water only. Avoid ice in street stalls. RO filtered water in restaurants is fine.

☐ **What is Delhi belly and how to avoid it?**
→ Traveller's diarrhea. Bottled water, avoid raw salads, eat at busy stalls (high turnover = fresh food), carry Imodium.

☐ **What should I pack for Delhi?**
→ Season-dependent. Winter: layers, it gets cold. Summer: light cotton. Always: modest clothing for temples, comfortable walking shoes, sunscreen.

☐ **Do I need a visa for India?**
→ Most nationalities need e-visa. Apply online 4+ days before. 30-day tourist visa available. Link to official site.

☐ **Is Uber available in Delhi?**
→ Yes. Uber and Ola both work. Download both apps. Need Indian SIM for OTP verification (get at airport).

☐ **How to get around Delhi?**
→ Metro (best), Uber/Ola (door to door), auto rickshaws (short trips, bargain first), avoid city buses and street taxis.

### PAA Pages
☐ **What food is Delhi famous for?**
→ Chaat, chole bhature, parathas (Paranthe Wali Gali), kebabs (Karim's), butter chicken (origin city), biryani, jalebis.

☐ **What is the best street food in Delhi?**
→ Top 10-15 dishes with where to find each. Golgappe, aloo tikki, dahi bhalle, ram ladoo, chhole kulche.

☐ **Where to eat in Old Delhi?**
→ Karim's (kebabs since 1913), Paranthe Wali Gali, Al Jawahar, Natraj Dahi Bhalle, Haji Mohd Hussain (fried chicken).

☐ **Is street food safe in Delhi?**
→ Mostly yes if you pick busy stalls. High turnover = fresh food. Avoid pre-cut fruit, tap water ice. Start mild, build tolerance.

☐ **How much does a food tour cost in Delhi?**
→ ₹1,500-3,500 ($18-42) for 3-4 hour walk. Includes 8-12 tastings. Private tours ₹3,000-6,000.

☐ **Which area in Delhi has the best food?**
→ Old Delhi (Chandni Chowk) for street food. Nizamuddin for Mughlai. Connaught Place for restaurants. Defence Colony for modern.

☐ **What is Paranthe Wali Gali?**
→ Famous alley in Chandni Chowk serving stuffed parathas since 1870s. 5-6 shops. Go morning for best experience.

☐ **Best restaurants in Delhi?**
→ Indian Accent (fine dining, contemporary), Bukhara (dal makhani, ITC Maurya), Karim's (Mughlai), SodaBottleOpenerWala (Parsi).

☐ **Best cooking classes in Delhi?**
→ Overview of options. Home cooking experiences, professional classes, market tour + cook combos. Price range ₹2,000-5,000.

☐ **Is Delhi vegetarian friendly?**
→ Extremely. Huge vegetarian culture. Most restaurants have extensive veg menus. Look for "pure veg" signs.

☐ **Where to drink craft beer in Delhi?**
→ Breweries: Bira 91 Taproom, Ek Bar, Cocktails and Dreams, Raasta. Beer prices ₹300-600 per pint.

☐ **Best cafes in Delhi?**
→ Indian Coffee House (Connaught Place, historic), Blue Tokai (speciality coffee), Kunzum Travel Cafe (Hauz Khas), Triveni Terrace Cafe.

☐ **What is butter chicken and where was it invented?**
→ Origin story: Kundan Lal Gujral, Moti Mahal, 1947. Where to eat authentic butter chicken in Delhi today.

☐ **Best biryani in Delhi?**
→ Al Jawahar (near Jama Masjid), Biryani By Kilo (delivery), Dum Pukht (ITC Maurya, luxury).

## CATEGORY 3: Heritage & History

### Category Page
☐ **Best Heritage Walks In Delhi — 2026 Guide**
- Walking tour options, self-guided routes, guided options
- Template: Category Page

### PAA Pages
☐ **How many heritage sites are in Delhi?**
→ 174 ASI-protected monuments. 3 UNESCO sites (Red Fort, Humayun's Tomb, Qutub Minar). More than any other Indian city.

☐ **What are the UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Delhi?**
→ Red Fort, Humayun's Tomb, Qutub Minar complex. Details on each, visiting tips.

☐ **How old is Delhi?**
→ Inhabited since 6th century BC (Indraprastha, Mahabharata). 7 historic cities of Delhi. Modern city founded 1911 (New Delhi).

☐ **What are the 7 cities of Delhi?**
→ Lal Kot, Siri, Tughlaqabad, Jahanpanah, Firozabad, Dinpanah/Shergarh, Shahjahanabad. Brief history of each.

☐ **Is Red Fort worth visiting?**
→ Yes but go early (opens 9:30, skip Mondays). Sound and light show evenings. ₹35 Indians, ₹500 foreigners.

☐ **How long do you need at Red Fort?**
→ 1.5-2 hours. Add 30 mins for museum. Combine with Chandni Chowk and Jama Masjid (all walkable).

☐ **Humayun's Tomb vs Taj Mahal — what's the difference?**
→ Humayun's Tomb came first (1570), inspired Taj Mahal (1653). Smaller, less crowded, arguably better for photos.

☐ **What is Agrasen ki Baoli?**
→ 14th century stepwell in Connaught Place. Free entry. Featured in PK (Bollywood film). Atmospheric, good for photography.

☐ **Is Qutub Minar worth visiting?**
→ Yes. 73m tall, 12th century, stunning carvings. ₹35/₹550. Go morning for best light. Combine with Mehrauli Archaeological Park.

☐ **Best free things to do in Delhi?**
→ Gurudwara Bangla Sahib, Lodhi Gardens, Lodhi Art District murals, Dilli Haat (₹30 entry), India Gate, Sunder Nursery.

☐ **What is Mehrauli Archaeological Park?**
→ 440 monuments in overgrown forest adjacent to Qutub Minar. Free entry. Rarely visited. One of Delhi's true hidden spots.

☐ **Best museums in Delhi?**
→ National Museum (free), National Gallery of Modern Art, Partition Museum, Crafts Museum, Gandhi Smriti.

☐ **Where to see Mughal architecture in Delhi?**
→ Red Fort, Humayun's Tomb, Jama Masjid, Purana Qila, Safdarjung's Tomb, Nizamuddin Dargah.

☐ **Self-guided walking tour Old Delhi?**
→ Route: Chandni Chowk metro → Red Fort → Chandni Chowk bazaar → Paranthe Wali Gali → Spice Market → Jama Masjid. 3-4 hours.

## CATEGORY 4: Markets & Shopping

### Category Page
☐ **Best Markets In Delhi — Shopping Guide 2026**
- All major markets, what each is known for, pricing, bargaining tips
- Template: Category Page

### PAA Pages
☐ **What are the best markets in Delhi?**
→ Chandni Chowk (everything), Khan Market (upscale), Sarojini Nagar (fashion bargains), Dilli Haat (handicrafts), Janpath (souvenirs).

☐ **What to buy in Delhi as souvenirs?**
→ Spices (Khari Baoli), textiles/scarves, miniature paintings, brass/copperware, Ayurvedic products, tea.

☐ **Is bargaining expected in Delhi markets?**
→ Yes in street markets (start at 50% of asking). No in malls/fixed-price shops. Dilli Haat has some room. Khan Market is fixed.

☐ **What is Dilli Haat?**
→ Open-air crafts bazaar. ₹30 entry. Handicrafts from all Indian states. Food stalls by state. INA metro station.

☐ **What is the biggest market in Delhi?**
→ Chandni Chowk — one of Asia's largest and oldest markets. Each lane specialises: textiles, electronics, spices, jewellery.

☐ **Where to buy spices in Delhi?**
→ Khari Baoli (Asia's largest spice market). Buy whole spices, freshly ground. Prices fraction of supermarket.

☐ **Best place to buy clothes in Delhi?**
→ Budget: Sarojini Nagar, Lajpat Nagar. Mid: Select Citywalk, Khan Market. Designer: Hauz Khas Village boutiques.

☐ **Is Khan Market expensive?**
→ Most expensive retail area in India. Boutiques, bookshops, cafes. Worth visiting for atmosphere even without buying.

### PAA Pages
☐ **Can you do a day trip from Delhi to Agra?**

☐ **Can you do a day trip from Delhi to Jaipur?**

☐ **Best weekend trips from Delhi?**
→ Agra, Jaipur, Rishikesh, Neemrana Fort, Mathura/Vrindavan, Bharatpur bird sanctuary, Jim Corbett.

☐ **How to get from Delhi to Agra?**
→ Train (Gatimaan Express, ₹750-1,500), car (3-4 hours, ₹3,000-4,000 return with driver), bus (not recommended).

☐ **Is Neemrana Fort worth visiting from Delhi?**
→ Yes, 2 hours drive. Stunning heritage hotel, zip-lining over the fort, pool, Rajasthani food. Day visit or overnight.

☐ **Day trip to Mathura and Vrindavan from Delhi?**
→ 3 hours drive. Krishna's birthplace. Temples, ghats, street food. Best during Holi (March).

## CATEGORY 7: Practical & Transport

### PAA Pages
☐ **How to use Delhi Metro?**
→ Buy token or get Metro card. Security check at every station. Women's carriage (first in direction of travel). Map + fare info.

☐ **Delhi Metro map and tips for tourists?**
→ Key stations: Rajiv Chowk (CP), Chandni Chowk, Kashmere Gate, Hauz Khas, Airport Express. ₹10-60 per journey.

☐ **Best SIM card for tourists in Delhi?**
→ Airtel or Jio. Buy at airport (₹500-700, includes data). Need passport photo + passport copy. Takes 2-24 hours to activate.

☐ **How to avoid scams in Delhi?**
→ Top scams: "your hotel is closed" taxi scam, gem scam, fake tourist office near New Delhi station. Ignore unsolicited help.

☐ **Do I need to tip in Delhi?**
→ 10% at restaurants if no service charge. Round up for auto drivers. ₹50-100 for guides/porters. Not expected at street food stalls.

☐ **What language do they speak in Delhi?**
→ Hindi primarily. English widely understood in tourist areas, hotels, restaurants. Some Punjabi and Urdu.

☐ **What currency does Delhi use?**
→ Indian Rupee (INR/₹). ATMs everywhere. Cards accepted at most restaurants/shops. Carry cash for markets/autos.

☐ **Is Delhi polluted?**
→ Yes, especially November-January (worst air quality globally). Check AQI before visiting. Wear N95 mask if >200 AQI.

☐ **What is the weather like in Delhi?**
→ Extreme seasons. Summers 45°C+, winters can drop to 2°C, monsoon July-Sept. Best: Oct-Mar.

## CATEGORY 9: Festivals & Seasonal

### PAA Pages
☐ **What festivals are celebrated in Delhi?**
→ Diwali (Oct/Nov), Holi (March), Republic Day (26 Jan), Dussehra (Oct), Eid, Christmas, Lohri (Jan). Calendar of dates.

☐ **Where to celebrate Holi in Delhi?**
→ Organised events safer for tourists than random street celebrations. Hotel-hosted Holi parties. Mathura day trip option.

☐ **What happens on Republic Day in Delhi?**
→ Military parade on Rajpath (Kartavya Path). 26 January. Tickets available online. Spectacular. Arrive very early.

☐ **Diwali in Delhi — what to expect?**
→ Festival of lights, Oct/Nov. Markets decorated, fireworks, sweets everywhere. Very loud. Air quality plummets.

☐ **What is Surajkund Mela?**
→ Annual crafts fair, February. Faridabad (30 min from Delhi). Handicrafts, performances, food from all states. ₹180 entry.

☐ **Best time to visit Delhi for festivals?**
→ October-March covers Dussehra, Diwali, Christmas, Republic Day, Surajkund Mela, Holi. Something every month.
