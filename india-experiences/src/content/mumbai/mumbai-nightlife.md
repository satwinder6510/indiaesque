**Mumbai's nightlife centers around three main districts: Bandra West for trendy pubs and rooftop bars, Lower Parel for upscale nightclubs, and Colaba for late-night casual spots. Expect entry fees of ₹1,500-3,000 ($18-36), strict dress codes, and age restrictions of 25+ at premium venues.**

Mumbai's party scene kicks off after 9 PM and reaches full swing by 11 PM. The city's nightlife reflects its cosmopolitan character, with everything from craft beer bars to high-energy dance clubs. Understanding the different districts helps you choose the right spot for your night out.

## Bandra West: The Hip Quarter

Bandra West leads Mumbai's nightlife scene with its concentration of trendy bars and pubs. Hill Road and Linking Road area house most venues, easily accessible via Bandra station.

**Top Spots:**
- **Toto's Garage**: Rock music bar with live bands, no entry fee, beer from ₹400 ($5)
- **Olive Bar & Kitchen**: Upscale Mediterranean restaurant-bar, cocktails ₹800-1,200 ($10-14)
- **Bonobo**: Craft beer specialist with 20+ taps, pints ₹450-650 ($5-8)
- **Brewbot Eatery**: Microbrewery with house-made beers, entry free on weekdays

Most Bandra West venues don't charge entry fees but maintain minimum spending requirements of ₹1,500-2,000 ($18-24) per person. The crowd skews younger (21-30) and more casual than Lower Parel's club scene.

## Lower Parel: Club Central

Lower Parel houses Mumbai's premium nightclub district, concentrated around Phoenix Mills and surrounding high-rises. Take the Western Railway to Lower Parel station.

**Premier Clubs:**
- **Tryst**: Mumbai's most exclusive club, entry ₹2,500-4,000 ($30-48), age 25+
- **Kitty Su**: High-energy dance club, international DJs, entry ₹2,000-3,500 ($24-42)
- **Toyroom**: Bollywood and commercial music, entry ₹1,800-3,200 ($22-38)
- **Lap**: Rooftop club with city views, entry ₹2,200-3,800 ($26-45)

Lower Parel clubs enforce strict age restrictions (25+), formal dress codes, and maintain guest list policies. Arrive before 10 PM for easier entry and lower cover charges.

## Colaba: Late-Night Casual Scene

Colaba offers the most relaxed nightlife atmosphere, with many venues staying open latest. The area around Colaba Causeway provides easy walking between spots.

**Key Venues:**
- **Leopold Cafe**: Iconic cafe-bar open until 1 AM, beer ₹300-400 ($4-5)
- **Cafe Mondegar**: Art-filled bar with jukebox, no entry fee
- **Harbour Bar**: Hotel bar at Taj, premium cocktails ₹1,000+ ($12+)
- **Colaba Social**: Co-working space by day, bar by night, cocktails ₹500-800 ($6-10)

Colaba venues typically have no entry fees and more lenient dress codes. The area stays lively until 2 AM, making it ideal for late-night bar hopping.

## Entry Fees and Costs

**Standard Entry Fees:**
- Bandra West bars: Free to ₹1,000 ($12)
- Lower Parel clubs: ₹1,500-4,000 ($18-48)
- Colaba venues: Free to ₹800 ($10)

**Drink Prices:**
- Local beer: ₹300-500 ($4-6)
- Premium beer: ₹500-800 ($6-10)
- Cocktails: ₹600-1,500 ($7-18)
- Premium spirits: ₹800-2,500 ($10-30)

Weekend prices increase by 20-30%. Many venues offer happy hour discounts from 6-8 PM.

## Dress Codes and Age Restrictions

**Men's Requirements:**
- Collared shirt or smart t-shirt
- Full-length pants (no shorts)
- Closed shoes (no flip-flops or sandals)
- Some venues require blazers after 10 PM

**Women's Guidelines:**
- Smart casual to cocktail attire
- Closed shoes preferred
- Avoid overly casual wear

Age restrictions vary by venue:
- Most bars: 21+
- Premium clubs: 25+
- Some exclusive venues: 28+

Carry government-issued photo ID always. Many venues use strict door policies and can refuse entry based on attire or group composition.

## Safety and Transportation

[Mumbai's safety for tourists](/mumbai/is-mumbai-safe-for-tourists/) extends to nightlife areas, but take standard precautions. The city's [transport system](/mumbai/transport/) runs limited services after midnight.

**Late-Night Transport:**
- Uber/Ola available 24/7, surge pricing after 1 AM
- Last local trains: 12:30 AM (Central), 1:15 AM (Western)
- Night buses run on select routes
- Pre-paid taxis from major hotels

**Safety Tips:**
- Stay in groups, especially women
- Keep valuables secure
- Use registered taxis or ride-sharing apps
- Avoid isolated areas after 2 AM
- Book accommodation in Bandra, Lower Parel, or South Mumbai for easier access

**Best Areas to Stay:**
Consider [where to stay in Mumbai](/mumbai/where-to-stay-mumbai/) based on nightlife preferences. Bandra West offers the most walkable nightlife scene, while staying in South Mumbai provides access to both Colaba and Lower Parel venues.

Peak nightlife season runs October through March when weather permits rooftop venues and outdoor seating. Plan your Mumbai nightlife as part of a comprehensive [Mumbai itinerary](/mumbai/how-many-days-mumbai/) to experience the city's diverse after-dark offerings.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What are the entry fees for Mumbai nightclubs?**

Most Mumbai nightclubs charge ₹1,500-3,000 ($18-36) entry fees, with premium venues like Tryst and Toyroom reaching ₹4,000 ($48) on weekends. Many bars in Bandra West have no cover charge but maintain minimum spend requirements.

**What's the dress code for Mumbai's nightlife venues?**

Smart casual to formal attire is required. Men need collared shirts, closed shoes, and full-length pants. Women should avoid flip-flops and overly casual wear. Upscale venues in Lower Parel enforce stricter dress codes.

**How late do Mumbai bars and clubs stay open?**

Most bars close at 1:30 AM on weekdays and 2:30 AM on weekends. Some restaurants with bar licenses serve alcohol until 1 AM. Only a few premium clubs have extended licenses until 3 AM on weekends.
