**Mumbai's best fine dining restaurants include Trishna (₹3,000-5,000), Bombay Canteen (₹2,500-4,000), and luxury hotel establishments like Wasabi and Golden Dragon at Taj Mahal Palace, offering everything from progressive Indian cuisine to authentic Japanese and Chinese dining experiences.**

## Top Fine Dining Restaurants in Mumbai

### Trishna - Contemporary Indian Seafood
Located in the historic Fort district near Horniman Circle, Trishna delivers exceptional contemporary Indian seafood. Chef Sujan Sarkar's menu features dishes like koliwada prawns with curry leaf mayo and Goan fish curry with coconut rice.

**Price Range:** ₹3,000-5,000 ($36-60) per person
**Cuisine:** Contemporary Indian, Seafood
**Address:** 7 Sai Baba Marg, Fort
**Reservations:** Essential, book 7-14 days ahead
**Dress Code:** Smart casual

### The Bombay Canteen - Modern Indian
This Kamala Mills restaurant revolutionized Mumbai's dining scene with its regional Indian approach. The menu changes seasonally, featuring dishes like Rajasthani laal maas and Bengali bhapa chingri. Their cocktail program incorporates Indian spices and ingredients.

**Price Range:** ₹2,500-4,000 ($30-48) per person
**Cuisine:** Modern Indian
**Address:** Kamala Mills, Lower Parel
**Reservations:** Required, especially weekends
**Dress Code:** Smart casual

### Taj Mahal Palace Hotel Restaurants

#### Wasabi - Japanese Fine Dining
Mumbai's premier Japanese restaurant offers an authentic experience with sushi master chefs from Japan. The omakase menu (₹5,500/$66) showcases seasonal ingredients flown in from Tokyo's Tsukiji market.

**Price Range:** ₹4,000-6,000 ($48-72) per person
**Cuisine:** Japanese
**Reservations:** Must book 10-14 days ahead
**Dress Code:** Formal

#### Golden Dragon - Cantonese Cuisine
This award-winning restaurant serves authentic Cantonese dishes in an elegant setting. Signature dishes include Peking duck and dim sum platters. The wine list features over 200 selections.

**Price Range:** ₹4,500-6,500 ($54-78) per person
**Cuisine:** Cantonese Chinese
**Reservations:** Essential
**Dress Code:** Formal

## Additional Fine Dining Options

### Khyber - North Indian
Operating since 1958, this Kala Ghoda institution serves refined North Indian cuisine in a colonial setting. Their dal Khyber and seekh kebabs are legendary among Mumbai food lovers.

**Price Range:** ₹2,800-4,200 ($34-50) per person
**Address:** 145 Mahatma Gandhi Road, Kala Ghoda

### Amadeus - European
Located in The Oberoi hotel, Amadeus offers European cuisine with Mediterranean influences. The restaurant features an open kitchen and extensive wine cellar.

**Price Range:** ₹4,000-5,500 ($48-66) per person
**Cuisine:** European, Mediterranean

## Reservation and Dining Tips

Most fine dining establishments require reservations 7-14 days in advance, particularly for weekend dinners. Call restaurants directly rather than relying on third-party apps for better table availability.

Dinner service typically starts at 7:30 PM, with last orders around 11 PM. Lunch service runs from 12:30-3:30 PM on weekdays.

If you're exploring Mumbai's food scene beyond fine dining, consider joining one of the [best food tours in Mumbai](/mumbai/food-tours/) to discover street food and local favorites. ## Wine and Service

Most fine dining restaurants have extensive wine lists with both imported and domestic selections. Expect a 300-400% markup on retail prices. Service charges (10-12%) are standard and added to your bill.

Tipping beyond the service charge is appreciated but not mandatory. Round up the bill or add ₹200-500 for exceptional service.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much should I budget for fine dining in Mumbai?
Expect to spend ₹2,500-5,000 ($30-60) per person at Mumbai's top fine dining restaurants. Trishna costs ₹3,000-5,000, Bombay Canteen ₹2,500-4,000, while Taj hotels like Wasabi and Golden Dragon range from ₹4,000-6,000 per person including drinks.

### Do Mumbai fine dining restaurants require reservations?
Yes, all top fine dining restaurants in Mumbai require advance reservations, especially Trishna, Wasabi, and Golden Dragon. Book 7-14 days ahead for weekends. Call directly or use their online booking systems. Walk-ins are rarely accommodated at premium establishments.

### What's the dress code for Mumbai's fine dining restaurants?
Smart casual to formal attire is required. Men need collared shirts and closed shoes - no shorts, flip-flops, or tank tops. Women should wear dresses, blouses with trousers/skirts, or elegant ethnic wear. Taj hotels are strictest about dress codes.
