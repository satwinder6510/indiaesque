**Kanheri Caves are 109 ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves dating from the 1st to 10th century CE, located inside Sanjay Gandhi National Park in Borivali, Mumbai. Entry costs ₹25 ($0.30) for Indians, with guided tours available and closure during monsoons.**

The Kanheri Caves represent one of Mumbai's most significant archaeological and spiritual sites. These caves served as a major Buddhist monastery complex for nearly a millennium, housing monks, students, and pilgrims who carved elaborate prayer halls, meditation chambers, and living quarters directly into the basalt rock face.

## Getting to Kanheri Caves

Reaching the caves requires planning since they're located deep within Sanjay Gandhi National Park. Take the Western Railway to Borivali station, then catch bus route 228 or hire an auto-rickshaw to the park's main gate in Borivali East. The journey from central Mumbai takes 45-60 minutes depending on traffic.

Once inside the national park, you'll need to take the park's van service since private vehicles aren't allowed beyond the entry point. The van costs ₹35 ($0.42) per person for the 5-kilometer journey to the cave site. Walking isn't recommended due to the distance and wildlife in the area.

For comprehensive guidance on navigating Mumbai's transport system, check our detailed [Mumbai Transport Guide](/mumbai/transport/).

## Entry Fees and Timings

Kanheri Caves charge separate entry fees from the national park. Indians pay ₹25 ($0.30) while foreign visitors pay ₹300 ($3.60). You'll also need to pay the Sanjay Gandhi National Park entry fee of ₹46 ($0.55) for adults and ₹23 ($0.28) for children.

The caves operate Tuesday to Sunday from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. They close on Mondays except during public holidays. Most importantly, the entire site shuts down during monsoon season from June to September due to safety concerns and difficult access roads.

Plan your visit during the cooler months between October and March for the most comfortable experience. The [best time to visit Mumbai](/mumbai/best-time-visit-mumbai/) coincides perfectly with optimal cave exploration weather.

## Exploring the Cave Complex

The 109 caves spread across the hillside in three main groups. Cave 1 serves as the main prayer hall (chaitya) with impressive stone pillars and a central stupa. This cave can accommodate over 300 people and showcases the architectural skills of ancient Buddhist craftsmen.

Cave 2 houses a massive statue of Buddha in teaching pose, while Cave 3 contains the most elaborate carvings including images of Buddha, Bodhisattvas, and various Buddhist symbols. The larger caves served as communal spaces, while smaller ones functioned as individual meditation cells for monks.

Don't miss Cave 34, which features a beautiful carved facade, and Cave 90, known for its water cistern system that collected rainwater for the monastery's daily needs. The caves demonstrate sophisticated engineering with drainage systems, water storage, and ventilation that kept the interiors cool.

## Guided Tours and Audio Guides

The Archaeological Survey of India provides guided tours in English, Hindi, and Marathi for ₹150 ($1.80) per group. These tours last approximately 2 hours and cover the historical significance, Buddhist symbolism, and architectural techniques used in cave construction.

Audio guides are available for ₹100 ($1.20) and provide detailed explanations for 15 major caves. The guides include fascinating details about monastic life, trade routes that funded the caves, and the gradual decline of Buddhism in the region.

Independent exploration works well too, as information boards in English and Hindi mark significant caves. Allow at least 3-4 hours to explore the main caves properly, including walking time between different sections.

## What to Bring and Expect

Wear comfortable walking shoes with good grip since cave floors can be uneven and sometimes slippery. Bring water bottles as the site has limited refreshment options. A flashlight or phone light helps illuminate darker cave interiors, though most major caves have adequate natural lighting.

The site involves considerable walking and climbing steps carved into rock faces. While not technically difficult, the terrain requires moderate fitness levels. Elderly visitors and those with mobility issues should assess their comfort with uneven stone steps and inclines.

Photography is allowed throughout the caves without additional charges. The interplay of natural light filtering through cave openings creates dramatic photo opportunities, especially in the larger prayer halls.

## Combining Your Visit

Kanheri Caves work excellently as part of a broader exploration of Mumbai's historical sites. Consider combining your visit with [heritage walks](/mumbai/heritage-walks/) in South Mumbai on different days to understand the city's complete historical timeline.

The caves typically take half a day to explore thoroughly. Factor this into your [overall Mumbai itinerary planning](/mumbai/how-many-days-mumbai/) since the location requires dedicated time and isn't easily combined with other city attractions on the same day.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to visit Kanheri Caves?

Entry to Kanheri Caves costs ₹25 ($0.30) for Indians and ₹300 ($3.60) for foreigners. You'll also need to pay Sanjay Gandhi National Park entry fees of ₹46 ($0.55) for adults and van transport within the park costs ₹35 ($0.42) per person.

### When are Kanheri Caves closed?

Kanheri Caves close during monsoon season from June to September for safety reasons. The caves are open Tuesday to Sunday from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. They remain closed on Mondays except public holidays.

### How do you reach Kanheri Caves from Mumbai?

Take the Western Railway to Borivali station, then catch bus route 228 or auto-rickshaw to Sanjay Gandhi National Park main gate. From there, board the park's van service to reach the caves, located 5km inside the park.
