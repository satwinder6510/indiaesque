Jaipur rewards every type of traveller — history buffs, shoppers, foodies, photographers. Here's your complete guide to the Pink City.

---

## Must-Do Experiences

### Watch Sunrise at Amber Fort

The morning light on Amber Fort's sandstone walls is striking. Arrive at 8 AM opening, before crowds and heat. Spend 2-3 hours exploring.

### Sunset from Nahargarh

The city of Jaipur spread below, turning pink in the evening light. Bring snacks and stay for twilight.

### Walk the Old City Bazaars

Johari Bazaar for jewels, Bapu Bazaar for textiles, Tripolia for bangles. The chaos, colours, and commerce are quintessential India.

### Photograph Hawa Mahal

The famous 953-window facade. Best photographed from Wind View Cafe across the street, morning light.

### Experience Chokhi Dhani

A recreated Rajasthani village with unlimited thali, folk performances, puppet shows, camel rides. Touristy but fun. Full evening experience.

## Best Photography Spots

### Sunrise/Morning

**Amber Fort** — Golden light on sandstone
**Panna Meena ka Kund** — Geometric stepwell near Amber
**City Palace courtyards** — Intricate patterns

### Sunset/Evening

**Nahargarh Fort** — City panorama
**Jal Mahal** — Water palace lit at night (view only, no entry)

### Any Time (with good light)

**Hawa Mahal** — From Wind View Cafe opposite
**Patrika Gate** — Colourful modern arch
**Albert Hall Museum** — Exterior architecture

### Tips

- Morning light (7-9 AM) is best
- Afternoon light is harsh
- Golden hour (5-6 PM) works for some spots
- Bring long lens for Amber Fort details

## Free Things to Do

### Temples

**Birla Mandir** — Modern white marble temple with city views

**Govind Dev Ji Temple** — Important Krishna temple, lively aarti

**Galta Ji (Monkey Temple)** — Ancient temple complex, hundreds of monkeys

### Markets

Walk the bazaars — the experience costs nothing (buying is optional)

### Street Life

- People-watch at Badi Chaupar (main square)
- Evening stroll on MI Road
- Watch artisans in old city

### Jal Mahal Views

The water palace is closed to visitors, but the lake view is free from the road. Beautiful at sunset.

## Activities & Experiences

### Cooking Classes

Learn to make dal bati churma, rajasthani curry. Market visit + cooking + eating. ₹2,500-5,000.

### Block Printing Workshop

Try traditional printing techniques. Make your own fabric. ₹1,500-3,000.

### Food Tours

Walk through old city with local guide, 8-12 tastings. ₹2,000-4,000.

### Heritage Walks

Guided walks through old city, understanding architecture and history. ₹1,000-2,500.

### Hot Air Balloon

Sunrise balloon ride over countryside. November-March only. ₹10,000-15,000.

## Day Trips

### Pushkar (3 hours)

Sacred lake, Brahma temple, hippie bazaar. Full day trip.

### Ranthambore (3.5 hours)

Tiger safari. Requires overnight for proper experience.

### Abhaneri (2 hours)

Chand Baori — one of India's deepest stepwells. Often combined with Fatehpur Sikri (on way to/from Agra).

### Ajmer (2.5 hours)

Dargah shrine, Taragarh Fort. Can combine with Pushkar.

## Seasonal Activities

### Winter (November-February)

- All outdoor activities
- Hot air balloon rides
- Comfortable fort exploration
- Literature Festival (January)

### Monsoon (July-September)

- Nahargarh views (dramatic clouds)
- Fewer crowds
- Some flooding possible
- Green countryside

### Summer (April-June)

- Indoor activities (museums, cooking classes)
- Early morning visits only
- Pool time at hotels
- Avoid midday sightseeing
