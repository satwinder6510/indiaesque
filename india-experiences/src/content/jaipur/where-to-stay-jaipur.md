**Choose Old City for heritage experience and monument access (₹3,000-15,000), C-Scheme for business travel and modern amenities (₹2,000-8,000), MI Road for shopping convenience (₹1,500-5,000), or Bani Park for budget stays (₹800-3,000).**

Each neighborhood in Jaipur offers distinct advantages depending on your travel style and budget. Your choice determines not just your accommodation cost but also your daily transport expenses and the overall feel of your Jaipur experience.

## Old City (Walled City)

The Old City puts you right in the heart of historic Jaipur, surrounded by pink sandstone walls built in 1727. You'll find heritage hotels converted from havelis (traditional mansions) and former royal residences.

**Price Range**: ₹3,000-15,000 ($36-180) per night

**Best For**: First-time visitors, heritage enthusiasts, photographers

**Key Advantages**:
- Walking distance to City Palace, Hawa Mahal, and Jantar Mantar
- Authentic Rajasthani architecture with courtyards and traditional decor
- Easy access to [Jaipur's best street food](/jaipur/best-street-food-jaipur/) in Johari Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar
- No daily transport costs to major attractions

**Transport Connectivity**: Limited vehicle access due to narrow lanes. Auto-rickshaws and cycle-rickshaws are your main transport options. The nearest railway station (Jaipur Junction) is 3 km away.

**Area Characteristics**: Expect bustling markets, narrow streets, and traditional architecture. Noise levels are high during daytime due to market activity. Wi-Fi can be inconsistent in older heritage properties.

## C-Scheme (Ashok Nagar)

C-Scheme serves as Jaipur's modern business district with corporate offices, upscale restaurants, and shopping centers. Hotels here cater to business travelers and tourists seeking contemporary amenities.

**Price Range**: ₹2,000-8,000 ($24-96) per night

**Best For**: Business travelers, families preferring modern amenities, luxury seekers

**Key Advantages**:
- 15 minutes to Jaipur Airport via taxi (₹300-400)
- Walking distance to World Trade Park and GT Central Mall
- Numerous ATMs, hospitals, and international restaurant chains
- Reliable internet connectivity and power backup

**Transport Connectivity**: Well-connected to all parts of Jaipur. City buses run frequently to Old City (₹10-15). Metro connectivity planned for 2026. Ola and Uber readily available.

**Area Characteristics**: Wide roads, modern infrastructure, and quieter than Old City. Limited local flavor but maximum convenience. Most hotels offer swimming pools and fitness centers.

## MI Road (Mirza Ismail Road)

MI Road stretches from railway station to C-Scheme, lined with shops, restaurants, and mid-range hotels. This area offers the best balance of accessibility and local experience.

**Price Range**: ₹1,500-5,000 ($18-60) per night

**Best For**: Shoppers, mid-budget travelers, those wanting central location

**Key Advantages**:
- Direct access to MI Road's shopping strip
- 2 km from railway station, easily walkable
- Close to Raj Mandir Cinema and popular eateries
- Good mix of budget and mid-range dining options

**Transport Connectivity**: Excellent bus connectivity to all major attractions. Share autos to Old City cost ₹10 per person. Cycle-rickshaws available for short distances (₹20-50).

**Area Characteristics**: Commercial atmosphere with mix of old and new buildings. Moderate noise levels. Street-side hotels may experience traffic sounds. Good for those wanting to experience [getting around Jaipur](/jaipur/getting-around-jaipur/) like locals.

## Bani Park

Bani Park developed as a residential area in the 1960s, now featuring numerous budget hotels and guesthouses. It's popular among backpackers and budget-conscious travelers.

**Price Range**: ₹800-3,000 ($10-36) per night

**Best For**: Budget travelers, backpackers, long-term stays

**Key Advantages**:
- Most affordable accommodation in decent condition
- Quiet residential neighborhood
- Several budget restaurants and local eateries
- Easy bargaining for auto-rickshaws and taxis

**Transport Connectivity**: 20-minute auto-rickshaw ride to Old City (₹80-100). Regular bus service to major attractions. Walking distance to Jaipur Railway Station (1.5 km).

**Area Characteristics**: Residential area with tree-lined streets. Limited nightlife and dining options compared to other areas. Most suitable for travelers who plan to spend entire days [exploring Jaipur's attractions](/jaipur/) and only need accommodation for sleeping.

## Transportation Costs from Each Area

**Daily transport budget estimates**:
- Old City: ₹0-200 ($0-2.4) - walking to main attractions
- C-Scheme: ₹300-500 ($3.6-6) - taxis to attractions
- MI Road: ₹150-300 ($1.8-3.6) - mix of buses and autos
- Bani Park: ₹200-400 ($2.4-4.8) - auto-rickshaws and buses

## Choosing Based on Your Travel Style

**Heritage seekers**: Book Old City heritage hotels for authentic experience and proximity to monuments.

**Comfort prioritizers**: Choose C-Scheme for modern amenities, reliable services, and airport connectivity.

**Budget travelers**: Bani Park offers clean, basic accommodation with good transport links.

**Shopping enthusiasts**: MI Road provides easy access to markets and balanced pricing.

Consider your [planned duration in Jaipur](/jaipur/how-many-days-jaipur/) when deciding. Shorter stays benefit from Old City's proximity to attractions, while longer visits may prefer C-Scheme's comfort and connectivity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Which area in Jaipur is best for first-time visitors?

Old City is ideal for first-time visitors due to proximity to major attractions like City Palace and Hawa Mahal. Heritage hotels here range from ₹3,000-15,000 ($36-180) and offer authentic Rajasthani architecture with easy walking access to monuments.

### What's the cheapest area to stay in Jaipur?

Bani Park offers the most budget-friendly accommodation with decent hotels starting from ₹800-3,000 ($10-36). It's well-connected to major attractions via auto-rickshaws and buses, making it perfect for backpackers and budget travelers.

### Is C-Scheme good for business travelers?

C-Scheme is Jaipur's business hub with modern hotels ranging ₹2,000-8,000 ($24-96). It offers excellent connectivity to the airport, railway station, and corporate offices, plus numerous restaurants and shopping malls within walking distance.
