**The easiest Bollywood experience in Delhi is watching a film at a local cinema — tickets cost ₹300-700 ($3.60-8.40), shows run all day, and you don't need to speak Hindi to enjoy the spectacle.** Beyond the movies, you can take a Bollywood dance class, catch a live musical show, or visit film-related landmarks around the city.

Delhi isn't Mumbai — this isn't where Bollywood films are made. But it's where a huge chunk of the audience lives, and the city's relationship with Hindi cinema is enthusiastic and deep. From the single-screen theatres of Old Delhi to the IMAX screens of South Delhi, there's a Bollywood experience to match every budget.

## Watch A Bollywood Film

### Where To Go

**PVR Select Citywalk, Saket** — The best multiplex experience in Delhi. Leather recliners, Dolby Atmos sound, and a mall attached for pre-movie snacking. Nearest metro: **Saket** (Yellow Line). Tickets ₹400-700 depending on the screen.

**PVR Plaza, Connaught Place** — A restored art-deco cinema on the inner circle of CP. It's been screening films since the 1950s and still has more character than any multiplex. Standard tickets ₹300-500. Walk from **Rajiv Chowk metro** (Blue/Yellow Line).

**PVR Priya, Vasant Vihar** — Another classic single-screen turned multiplex. Popular with the South Delhi crowd. Tickets ₹350-600.

**Raj Mandir style experience:** If you want the old-school single-screen vibe — audience reactions, interval samosas, and all — look for screenings at **Delite Cinema** near Delhi Gate or **Regal Cinema** (if reopened) near CP. These are rougher around the edges but far more fun than a sanitised multiplex.

### What To Know

You don't need to understand Hindi. Bollywood films are 60% visual spectacle — the song-and-dance sequences, the fight scenes, the dramatic reveals all work regardless of language. The audience around you will do half the work: cheering, whistling, throwing popcorn. That's the experience.

**Book online** through BookMyShow or the PVR app. Weekend evening shows sell out for big releases. Weekday matinees (12-2pm) are cheap and empty — ₹200-300.

**Interval is real.** Bollywood films are 2.5-3 hours long and always have a 15-minute interval halfway through. This is when you get samosas, popcorn, and chai from the lobby. Don't skip it — it's part of the culture.

This is also a solid option on a [rainy day in Delhi](/delhi/things-to-do-in-delhi-when-it-rains/) when outdoor plans fall apart.

## Take A Bollywood Dance Class

Several studios in Delhi offer tourist-friendly Bollywood dance workshops. These aren't ongoing courses — they're standalone 1-2 hour sessions designed for first-timers.

**Delhi Dance Academy** — Lajpat Nagar. 90-minute group workshops for ₹800-1,200 ($10-14.50). The instructor picks a current hit song and breaks down the choreography. Groups of 5-15 people. No dance background needed. They can arrange private sessions for ₹2,000-3,000 if you want a more personalised class.

**Natya Social** — South Delhi (location varies). Dance-and-dine events where you learn Bollywood choreography in a restaurant setting, then eat. ₹1,200-1,500 ($14.50-18) including food and one drink.

**Hotel-arranged workshops** — Many upscale hotels (The Imperial, ITC Maurya, Taj Palace) can arrange a private Bollywood dance session with a choreographer for hotel guests. Expect to pay ₹3,000-5,000 per person for a 1-hour private session.

The best workshops teach you one complete routine to a specific song. By the end, you have a video of yourself doing the whole thing — good for embarrassing yourself on social media later.

## Kingdom Of Dreams — Gurgaon

Kingdom of Dreams is a large-scale entertainment venue in **Sector 29, Gurgaon** (now Gurugram) that hosts Bollywood-style musical theatre shows. Think Broadway meets Bollywood — live singing, elaborate sets, 50+ performers, flying rigs, and theatrical effects.

**Shows:** Zangoora (adventure musical) and Jhumroo (romantic comedy). Both run 2-2.5 hours with interval. Performed in Hindi with enough visual storytelling that non-Hindi speakers follow easily.

**Tickets:** ₹1,500-4,000 ($18-48) depending on seating category. Book through their website or BookMyShow.

**Getting there:** Take the **Yellow Line metro to HUDA City Centre**, then a 10-minute auto-rickshaw. Or cab from central Delhi — 45 minutes to an hour depending on traffic.

**Important:** Kingdom of Dreams has faced intermittent closures in recent years. Check their website or call +91-124-452-8000 before making the trip.

The venue also has a food court called "Culture Gully" themed around different Indian states. It's touristy but fun, especially if you're going with kids.

## Bollywood Landmarks In Delhi

A few Delhi locations regularly appear in Bollywood films:

- **India Gate** — backdrop for countless patriotic scenes
- **Connaught Place** — the circular market shows up in romantic sequences
- **Qutub Minar** — featured in films from the 1960s onwards
- **Delhi University North Campus** — classic college-romance shooting location

You won't find organised "Bollywood location tours" in Delhi the way you would in Mumbai, but knowing the connections adds a layer when you visit these spots. For more on [Delhi's nightlife](/delhi/best-nightlife-in-delhi/), many of the bars and clubs play Bollywood music heavily — especially on weekends.

## FAQ

### Can I watch a Bollywood movie in a cinema in Delhi?

Yes. PVR and INOX cinemas across Delhi screen the latest Bollywood releases daily. PVR at Select Citywalk (Saket) and PVR Plaza (Connaught Place) are the most popular. Tickets cost ₹300-700 ($3.60-8.40) depending on the screen and timing. No Hindi needed — the music, dance sequences, and drama translate across languages.

### Where can I take a Bollywood dance class in Delhi?

Delhi Dance Academy in Lajpat Nagar and Natya Social in South Delhi offer Bollywood dance workshops for tourists. Classes run 1-2 hours and cost ₹800-1,500 ($10-18). You'll learn choreography from recent hit songs. No dance experience needed — the instructors break everything down step by step.

### Is Kingdom of Dreams still open?

Kingdom of Dreams in Gurgaon has had intermittent closures. Check their website or call ahead before visiting. When open, it hosts Zangoora and Jhumroo — large-scale Bollywood-style musical theatre shows with lavish sets, costumes, and dance numbers. Tickets start at ₹1,500 ($18). It's about 45 minutes from central Delhi by metro or cab.
