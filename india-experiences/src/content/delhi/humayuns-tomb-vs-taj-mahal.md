**Humayun's Tomb (1570) came 83 years before the Taj Mahal (1653) and directly inspired its design — the Taj is the famous descendant, but Humayun's Tomb is the original.** Both are Mughal garden tombs built by grieving spouses, both are UNESCO World Heritage Sites, and both are worth your time for different reasons.

## The Architectural Connection

The relationship between these two buildings is not subtle. Humayun's Tomb introduced several architectural ideas that Shah Jahan would later perfect at the Taj Mahal:

**The char bagh garden:** Humayun's Tomb sits at the centre of a formal garden divided into four quadrants by water channels, a design rooted in Persian descriptions of paradise. The Taj Mahal uses the same layout but on a grander scale.

**The double dome:** The tomb has an inner dome (visible from the burial chamber) and an outer dome (creating the exterior profile). This engineering solution — allowing a tall, dramatic exterior without an impossibly high interior — was new to Indian architecture. The Taj Mahal refined it with a bulbous onion dome that became the defining shape of Mughal architecture.

**Symmetry and axiality:** Both buildings sit on raised platforms at the centre of perfectly symmetrical grounds. The approach is designed to frame the tomb as you walk toward it.

**Material choices:** Humayun's Tomb combines red sandstone with white marble accents. The Taj Mahal flipped this, using white marble as the primary material with sandstone for subsidiary buildings. The shift from sandstone to marble is one of the clearest markers of how Mughal aesthetics evolved over those 83 years.

## How They Compare in Practice

| | Humayun's Tomb | Taj Mahal |
|--|----------------|-----------|
| **Built** | 1570 | 1653 |
| **Built by** | Bega Begum (for Emperor Humayun) | Shah Jahan (for Mumtaz Mahal) |
| **Location** | Nizamuddin East, Delhi | Agra (230 km from Delhi) |
| **Primary material** | Red sandstone with white marble | White marble with sandstone |
| **Entry fee (foreigners)** | ₹550 ($6.50) | ₹1,100 ($13) |
| **Crowd level** | Moderate — manageable even midday | Intense — 7-8 million visitors per year |
| **Photography** | Easy — fewer people, open sightlines | Challenging — constant crowds in frame |
| **Time needed** | 1-1.5 hours | 2-3 hours |
| **Travel from Delhi** | 30 minutes by metro/auto | 3-4 hours by car or 1h40m by train |

## Why Humayun's Tomb Has Advantages

**Fewer people, better photos.** The Taj Mahal receives around 20,000-30,000 visitors on peak days. Humayun's Tomb gets a fraction of that. You can photograph the tomb from the char bagh gardens without a single person in the frame, especially on weekday mornings. At the Taj, getting a clean shot requires either a 5 AM entry or serious patience.

**Lower cost and no travel.** If you're based in Delhi, Humayun's Tomb is a 30-minute auto ride from Connaught Place. The Taj Mahal requires either a full [day trip to Agra](/delhi/can-you-do-a-day-trip-from-delhi-to-agra/) or an overnight stay.

**The surrounding complex.** Humayun's Tomb is not a standalone monument. The complex includes Isa Khan's octagonal tomb (1547), the Arab Serai, the Barber's Tomb, the Nila Gumbad, and the recently restored Bu Halima Garden. Adjacent Sunder Nursery adds another 90 acres of restored Mughal gardens with 15 monuments. You could spend half a day here.

**Nizamuddin neighbourhood.** The tomb is in [Nizamuddin](/delhi/things-to-do-in-nizamuddin/), one of Delhi's oldest neighbourhoods. Walk 10 minutes south to the Nizamuddin Dargah, the 14th-century Sufi shrine where qawwali musicians perform on Thursday evenings. The streets around the dargah have some of Delhi's best kebab shops.

## Why the Taj Mahal Still Wins on Impact

Let's be honest: the Taj Mahal is one of the most beautiful buildings humans have ever constructed. The white marble changes colour through the day — pink at dawn, white at noon, golden at sunset. The pietra dura inlay work (semiprecious stones set into the marble) is on a level of detail that photographs cannot capture. And the sheer scale of it — the 73-metre-high dome, the four minarets, the reflecting pool — creates an emotional response that Humayun's Tomb, for all its elegance, doesn't quite match.

If you're visiting India and can only see one, the Taj Mahal is the choice. But if you're already in Delhi, skipping Humayun's Tomb to save time is a mistake.

## The Best Approach: See Both

See Humayun's Tomb first. It'll take you 60-90 minutes and costs ₹550 ($6.50) for foreign visitors. You'll understand the garden tomb concept, the Mughal architectural vocabulary, and the double dome structure. Then, when you see the Taj Mahal — whether as a day trip from Delhi or on a longer Agra visit — you'll appreciate how Shah Jahan's architects took Humayun's prototype and pushed every element to its extreme.

For more on Delhi's Mughal heritage, join one of the [heritage walks](/delhi/heritage-walks/) that cover the Nizamuddin-Humayun's Tomb circuit. The guides connect this architecture to the broader story of Mughal Delhi in a way that reading plaques never will.

## Is Humayun's Tomb a Copy of the Taj Mahal?

It's the other way around. Humayun's Tomb was completed in 1570, a full 83 years before the Taj Mahal (1653). The architectural concepts pioneered here — a central tomb in a symmetrical char bagh garden, the double dome, the use of white marble with red sandstone — directly influenced Shah Jahan's design for the Taj. Humayun's Tomb is the prototype; the Taj Mahal is the refinement.

## Should I Visit Humayun's Tomb if I'm Also Going to See the Taj Mahal?

Yes. Seeing Humayun's Tomb first actually deepens your experience of the Taj Mahal, because you can trace how Mughal architecture evolved over 83 years. The two buildings have a clear family resemblance but differ in material, scale, and detail. Humayun's Tomb is also far less crowded and easier to photograph without hundreds of people in your frame.

## Can I Do a Day Trip From Delhi to See the Taj Mahal?

Yes. The Gatimaan Express leaves Hazrat Nizamuddin station at 6:10 AM and reaches Agra in 1 hour 40 minutes. You'll have time for the Taj Mahal and Agra Fort before the 5:50 PM return train. By car via the Yamuna Expressway, it's 3-4 hours each way. Book the train at least two weeks ahead — it sells out.
