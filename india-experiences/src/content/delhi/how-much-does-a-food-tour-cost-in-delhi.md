**A group food tour in Delhi costs ₹1,500-3,500 ($18-42) per person, lasts 3-4 hours, and includes 8-12 tastings at different stalls and restaurants.** Private food tours run ₹3,000-6,000 ($36-72) per person. Most tours cover Old Delhi's Chandni Chowk area, which has the highest concentration of legendary food stalls.

## Group Food Tour Pricing

Group tours run with 4-12 people and are the most common format:

| Tour Type | Price Range | Duration | Tastings |
|-----------|------------|----------|----------|
| Budget group tour | ₹1,500-2,000 ($18-24) | 2.5-3 hours | 6-8 stops |
| Standard group tour | ₹2,000-3,000 ($24-36) | 3-4 hours | 8-12 stops |
| Premium group tour | ₹3,000-3,500 ($36-42) | 4-5 hours | 10-15 stops |

The price difference between budget and premium comes down to the number of tastings, whether a rickshaw ride is included, and the size of the group. Smaller groups (4-6 people) tend to cost more but move faster through crowded lanes.

## Private Food Tour Pricing

Private tours for 1-4 people run ₹3,000-6,000 ($36-72) per person. The advantages are real: you set the pace, the guide adjusts to your spice tolerance and dietary restrictions, and you can linger at stalls you love or skip ones that don't appeal. For couples or families with kids, private tours are often worth the premium.

Some operators offer private tours for a flat fee of ₹8,000-15,000 ($96-180) for the group regardless of size, which works out cheaper per person if you're travelling with friends.

## What's Included

Most [food tours](/delhi/food-tours/) include:

- **All food tastings** — you don't pay at individual stalls
- **Bottled water** — essential in Delhi's heat
- **A local guide** — usually someone who grew up eating at these stalls
- **Walking transport** between stops
- **Some tours add** a cycle rickshaw ride through Old Delhi's lanes

Not typically included: tips for the guide (₹200-500 per person is standard), additional food or drinks you decide to buy, and transport to/from the meeting point.

## Where Tours Usually Go

The vast majority of Delhi food tours focus on **Old Delhi**, specifically the lanes around Chandni Chowk, Jama Masjid, and Chawri Bazaar. This is where the density of historic food stalls is highest. A typical route might include:

1. Paranthe Wali Gali for stuffed parathas
2. A chaat stall for dahi bhalle or golgappe
3. Karim's or a similar kebab house near Jama Masjid
4. Old Famous Jalebi Wala for hot jalebis
5. A spice market visit in Khari Baoli
6. Kulfi at Kuremal's in Chawri Bazaar

Some operators also run South Delhi food tours covering **Defence Colony**, **CR Park** (Bengali food), and **Nizamuddin** — these tend to focus more on sit-down restaurants and cost slightly more.

For the full breakdown of what to eat in the area, read our [Old Delhi eating guide](/delhi/where-to-eat-in-old-delhi/).

## Best Time to Book

Morning tours (starting 9-10 AM) are best for Old Delhi. The stalls are freshly stocked, the oil is clean, and the heat hasn't peaked yet. Evening tours (starting 5-6 PM) work too but get more crowded.

Avoid booking on **Tuesdays** — several historic shops in Chandni Chowk close that day. Also avoid **Friday afternoons** near Jama Masjid due to prayer closures.

Book at least 2-3 days in advance for group tours. Private tours often need a week's notice during peak season (October to March).

## How to Choose a Tour

Look for these things:

- **Group size cap** — anything over 10 people gets unwieldy in Old Delhi's lanes
- **Specific stall names listed** — vague descriptions like "visit a famous kebab shop" are a red flag; good operators name their stops
- **Vegetarian options** — most operators accommodate this, but confirm in advance
- **Guide credentials** — the best guides are food writers, culinary students, or locals who grew up in Old Delhi

Check the [best street food in Delhi](/delhi/what-is-the-best-street-food-in-delhi/) before your tour so you know what to expect and can ask your guide informed questions.

## What Is Included in a Delhi Food Tour?

Most Delhi food tours include 8-12 food tastings at different stalls and restaurants, bottled water, a local guide, and walking transport between stops. Some include rickshaw rides through Old Delhi's lanes. Food is the main cost — your tastings are pre-paid, so you don't need cash at each stall. Tips for the guide are extra, usually ₹200-500 per person.

## Are Delhi Food Tours Worth the Money?

Yes, especially for first-time visitors. The stalls that guides take you to are often inside unmarked lanes you'd never find alone. You also eat a wider range of dishes in 3-4 hours than you'd manage in days of solo exploring. The guide handles ordering, explains what you're eating, and knows which stalls have the best hygiene. After one guided tour, you'll feel confident eating on your own.

## Can I Do a Vegetarian Food Tour in Delhi?

Absolutely. Most Delhi food tour operators offer vegetarian-specific routes or can adapt their standard tour. Delhi's street food scene is heavily vegetarian — chole bhature, parathas, dahi bhalle, golgappe, jalebis, and kulfi are all meat-free. A vegetarian food tour in Old Delhi covers just as much ground and variety as the non-vegetarian version.
