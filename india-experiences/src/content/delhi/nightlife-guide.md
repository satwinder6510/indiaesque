Delhi's nightlife runs from Sufi devotional music in medieval shrines to rooftop cocktail bars overlooking the city. Here's where to go after dark.

---

## Best Nightlife Areas

### Hauz Khas Village

**Vibe:** Delhi's densest bar concentration. Trendy, young, artsy crowd.

**What's there:**
- 20+ bars and lounges packed into narrow lanes
- Rooftops with lake views (medieval tank visible)
- Mix of cocktail bars, beer joints, and dance clubs

**Best venues:**
- **Raasta** — Reggae-themed, casual atmosphere
- **Social** — Co-working by day, bar by night. Great vibe.
- **Mia Bella** — Italian restaurant with bar, rooftop
- **Imperfecto** — Quirky decor, cocktails

**Hours:** Most open around 12 PM, bars active from 7 PM, peak 10 PM - 1 AM

**Getting there:** Metro to Hauz Khas (Yellow/Magenta), then 10-minute walk or auto

**Tips:**
- Weekends get packed after 10 PM
- Cover charges common at some venues (₹1,000-2,000, often redeemable on drinks)
- Dress code: smart casual, no flip-flops

### Connaught Place

**Vibe:** Mainstream, mixed crowd, more accessible than Hauz Khas

**Best spots:**
- **N Block:** Warehouse Cafe, Ardor, Colocal
- **M Block:** Unplugged Courtyard, Lord of the Drinks
- **Inner Circle:** Farzi Cafe, The Wine Company

**What to expect:**
- Larger venues, more dance floors
- Cover charges on weekends
- Mix of local professionals and tourists
- Live music at some venues

**Getting there:** Rajiv Chowk Metro (Yellow/Blue Line)

### Aerocity

**Vibe:** Upscale, hotel-adjacent, international feel

**Best spots:**
- **JW Marriott:** K3, Adrift Kaya
- **Andaz Delhi:** Annamaya, Juniper Bar
- **General:** Molecule Air Bar, Street Delhi

**What to expect:**
- Higher prices than central Delhi
- Well-dressed crowd
- Strong cocktail programs
- Easy airport access for late arrivals

**Getting there:** Airport Express Metro to Aerocity

### Mehrauli

**Vibe:** Upscale, destination dining/drinking near Qutub Minar

**Best spots:**
- **Ek Bar** — Excellent cocktails, modern Indian bar
- **Olive Bar & Kitchen** — Mediterranean, beautiful courtyard
- **Qla** — Rooftop with Qutub Minar views

**What to expect:**
- Older, more sophisticated crowd
- Better cocktail programs
- Higher prices
- Need car/Uber to reach

## Things to Do at Night

### Qawwali at Nizamuddin Dargah

**What:** Free Sufi devotional music at a 700-year-old shrine

**When:** Every Thursday after Maghrib (sunset) prayers, around 7-8 PM

**Where:** Nizamuddin Dargah, Nizamuddin West

**What to expect:**
- Qawwals (musicians) perform in the courtyard
- Powerful, hypnotic music
- Spiritual atmosphere
- Crowded but welcoming

**Tips:**
- Arrive by 6:30 PM for good position
- Dress modestly, women cover heads
- Remove shoes
- Give small donation to performers after
- Combine with dinner at Ghalib Kabab Corner

**Metro:** Hazrat Nizamuddin (Pink Line)

### Illuminated Monuments

**India Gate:** Always lit, pleasant for an evening walk. Street food vendors nearby. Best 7-10 PM.

**Rashtrapati Bhavan:** Beautiful when illuminated. Drive along Kartavya Path for the view.

**Lotus Temple:** Can't enter at night but the exterior is illuminated. Worth a drive-by.

### Late-Night Food

**Old Delhi (until 10 PM):**
- Karim's kebabs
- Aslam Chicken Corner
- Street chaat

**Pandara Road (until 11 PM):**
- Gulati
- Pindi
- Have More

**Connaught Place (until midnight):**
- Saravana Bhavan
- Zen
- Various restaurants

**24-Hour options:**
- McDonalds, Dominos (chain basics)
- Some hotel restaurants

### Night Drives

The city looks different at night — less traffic, monuments illuminated, cooler temperatures.

**Scenic routes:**
- India Gate → Rashtrapati Bhavan → Parliament (Kartavya Path)
- Qutub Minar approach (lit at night)
- Diplomatic Enclave — wide, tree-lined streets

## Practical Information

### Alcohol Laws

- **Legal drinking age:** 25 in Delhi (strictly enforced at upscale venues)
- **Dry days:** National holidays, election days — no alcohol served anywhere
- **Hours:** Bars close by 1 AM (some exceptions in hotels)
- **Cover charges:** ₹1,000-3,000 at clubs, often redeemable

### Dress Code

- **Clubs:** Smart casual minimum. No shorts, flip-flops, sleeveless for men
- **Lounges:** Smart casual
- **Casual bars:** Anything clean and presentable

### Couples Policy

Many clubs have "couples only" entry — single men may be denied or charged higher cover. This is standard practice.

### Safety

- Use Uber/Ola to get home
- Stick to known areas
- Hauz Khas lanes can get sketchy late — main roads are fine
- See our [safety guide](/delhi/safety-guide/) for details

## Best Nights

**Wednesday:** Some bars have ladies' night deals

**Thursday:** Qawwali at Nizamuddin, plus start of weekend

**Friday/Saturday:** Busiest, most expensive, longest queues

**Sunday:** Quieter, some venues closed

## Beyond Delhi: Gurugram (Gurgaon)

Delhi's nightlife extends to Gurugram, 30-45 minutes south. More options, bigger venues, later hours.

**Best areas:**
- **Cyber Hub** — Multiple restaurants and bars in one complex
- **Golf Course Road** — Upscale lounges
- **Sector 29** — Budget-friendly options

**Worth the trip for:** Dedicated craft breweries, larger clubs, Sunday brunches

**Getting there:** Metro to Huda City Centre, then auto/Uber
