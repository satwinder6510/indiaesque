**Eco Park New Town is Kolkata's largest urban park spanning 480 acres in New Town, with entry fees of ₹30 ($0.35) weekdays and ₹50 ($0.60) weekends. Located 21km from the city center, this recreational space features multiple themed zones, boating, cycling, and requires half a day to explore properly.**

This sprawling park opened in 2013 and has become one of the top [attractions in Kolkata](/kolkata/) for families and nature lovers. You'll find well-maintained gardens, artificial lakes, adventure activities, and plenty of open spaces perfect for picnics and relaxation.

## Getting to Eco Park

Eco Park sits in New Town, approximately 21km northeast of central Kolkata. Take the Metro Blue Line to New Town station, then catch an auto-rickshaw for ₹50-80 ($0.60-0.95) to reach the main gate. If you're driving, follow Major Arterial Road (MAR) or Central Park Road - GPS coordinates are 22.5726°N, 88.4755°E.

The park provides ample parking space for ₹20 ($0.25) for two-wheelers and ₹50 ($0.60) for cars. Consider visiting during your [recommended stay duration](/kolkata/how-many-days-visit-kolkata/) to fit this experience into your Kolkata itinerary.

## Park Zones and Attractions

### Theme Gardens
The park divides into several themed sections. The Urban Forest recreates a natural woodland environment with over 100 tree species. The Bamboo Garden showcases different bamboo varieties in landscaped settings. The Rose Garden blooms with colorful varieties, particularly beautiful during winter months.

### Water Activities
The central lake offers paddle boating for ₹100 ($1.20) per 30 minutes for a two-person boat. Row boats cost ₹80 ($0.95) for the same duration. The lake also features a musical fountain show every evening at 7 PM and 8 PM.

### Adventure Zone
Zip-lining costs ₹150 ($1.80) per ride and covers 200 meters over the lake. The rock climbing wall charges ₹100 ($1.20) per attempt. Burma Bridge and rope courses are available for ₹80 ($0.95) each.

### Family Areas
The Children's Park includes swings, slides, and jungle gyms. A toy train runs on weekends for ₹30 ($0.35) per ride, covering major park sections in 20 minutes.

## Cycling and Walking Trails

Rent bicycles at the main entrance for ₹50 ($0.60) per hour. The park maintains separate cycling tracks that wind through different zones safely. Walking trails total approximately 8km if you cover the entire perimeter.

Tandem bikes cost ₹80 ($0.95) per hour, while family cycles accommodating four people charge ₹120 ($1.45) hourly. Helmets are provided free with all rentals.

## Food and Refreshments

Multiple food courts serve Bengali, North Indian, Chinese, and fast food options. The main food court near the entrance offers meals ranging from ₹150-400 ($1.80-4.80). Smaller kiosks throughout the park sell snacks, ice cream, and beverages for ₹20-100 ($0.25-1.20).

You can bring your own food and drinks. Designated picnic areas with tables and benches are scattered throughout the park, making it perfect for family gatherings.

## Best Times to Visit

Visit during winter months (November-February) when Kolkata's [weather is most pleasant](/kolkata/best-time-visit-kolkata/). The park opens daily from 10 AM to 8 PM, with last entry at 7 PM.

Weekday visits offer lower entry fees and fewer crowds. Early morning (10-11 AM) or late afternoon (4-6 PM) provide the best weather conditions and lighting for photography.

Avoid visiting during monsoon season (June-September) as many outdoor activities remain closed due to rain.

## Planning Your Visit

Allocate half a day (4-6 hours) for a complete Eco Park experience. Wear comfortable walking shoes and bring a water bottle. Sun protection is essential during summer months.

The park integrates well with other New Town attractions, allowing you to explore the planned city area. Consider combining your visit with shopping at nearby malls or dining at New Town's restaurants.

[Transportation options](/kolkata/kolkata-transport-guide/) from different parts of Kolkata make reaching Eco Park straightforward, though journey times vary based on your starting location.

## Facilities and Amenities

Clean restrooms are available at regular intervals throughout the park. First aid stations operate near major activity zones. ATMs are located at the main entrance and central food court area.

The park maintains wheelchair accessibility on main pathways, though some adventure activities have restrictions. Photography is allowed everywhere except in restricted administrative areas.

Security guards patrol regularly, and CCTV cameras monitor all major zones, ensuring visitor safety throughout your visit.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What are the entry fees for Eco Park New Town?**
Entry to Eco Park costs ₹30 ($0.35) on weekdays and ₹50 ($0.60) on weekends. Additional charges apply for specific activities like boating, cycling, and rides within different zones.

**How much time do you need at Eco Park?**
Plan for half a day (4-6 hours) to explore Eco Park properly. This gives you enough time to visit multiple zones, enjoy activities like boating or cycling, and have meals at the food courts.

**Is Eco Park suitable for families with children?**
Yes, Eco Park is extremely family-friendly with dedicated children's play areas, safe cycling tracks, paddle boats, toy trains, and multiple food courts. The park has something for every age group.
