**The best areas to stay in Kolkata are Park Street/Esplanade for central access to attractions (₹2,000-5,000/$24-60 per night), Sudder Street for budget travelers (₹800-2,000/$10-24), Salt Lake for modern amenities (₹3,000-8,000/$36-96), and the Airport area for transit stays (₹2,500-6,000/$30-72).**

Choosing where to stay in Kolkata depends on your budget, travel style, and what you want to see. The city's accommodation ranges from backpacker hostels to luxury heritage hotels, each concentrated in specific neighborhoods with distinct advantages.

## Park Street and Esplanade: The Heart of Kolkata

Park Street and the nearby Esplanade area put you at the center of everything. You can walk to Victoria Memorial, St. Paul's Cathedral, and the Indian Museum. The Park Street metro station connects you to other parts of the city within minutes.

Mid-range hotels here charge ₹2,000-3,500 ($24-42) per night. The Kenilworth Hotel on Little Russell Street offers colonial charm at ₹4,500 ($54), while Hotel Hindusthan International on Jawaharlal Nehru Road provides modern amenities at ₹3,200 ($38). Budget options like Hotel Galaxy on Mirza Ghalib Street start at ₹2,000 ($24).

The area buzzes with restaurants, bars, and cafes. You're steps away from Flury's tearoom and Peter Cat restaurant. [Getting Around Kolkata](/kolkata/kolkata-transport-guide/) becomes easier from this central location, with taxis and metros readily available.

## Sudder Street: Backpacker Central

Sudder Street has been Kolkata's backpacker hub for decades. This short street near New Market offers the city's cheapest accommodation, with dormitory beds from ₹800 ($10) and private rooms from ₹1,500 ($18) per night.

The Salvation Army Red Shield Guest House provides clean, basic rooms at ₹1,200 ($14). Hotel Maria offers slightly more comfort at ₹1,800 ($22). For the absolute budget traveler, Modern Lodge has dormitory-style accommodation starting at ₹800 ($10).

Sudder Street sits within walking distance of the Indian Museum and New Market. The area has numerous budget restaurants serving Indian and continental food. Travel agencies line the street, making it easy to book tours or onward travel. Despite being budget-focused, the area remains [safe for tourists](/kolkata/is-kolkata-safe-for-tourists/).

## Salt Lake and New Town: Modern Kolkata

Salt Lake (officially Bidhannagar) and New Town offer Kolkata's modern face. Wide roads, planned layouts, shopping malls, and newer infrastructure define these areas. Hotels here range from ₹3,000-8,000 ($36-96) per night.

The Swissotel Kolkata in New Town charges ₹6,500 ($78) for contemporary luxury. Hotel Polo Towers in Salt Lake offers business-friendly amenities at ₹4,200 ($50). Budget options like Hotel Sonar Bangla start at ₹3,000 ($36).

Salt Lake connects to central Kolkata via the blue metro line, taking 20-30 minutes to reach Park Street. The area has City Centre mall, numerous restaurants, and a more suburban feel. Choose this area if you prefer modern amenities over historic charm, but expect longer commutes to [heritage sites](/kolkata/heritage-sites/).

## Airport Area: Transit and Business Hub

The airport area works well for early flights or business travelers visiting the nearby IT corridor. Hotels here charge ₹2,500-6,000 ($30-72) per night and often include airport transfers.

The Airport Hotel Kolkata offers basic comfort at ₹3,200 ($38) with free airport pickup. For luxury, the ITC Sonar charges ₹5,800 ($70) and features extensive amenities including a spa and multiple restaurants. Budget options like Hotel Royal Castle start at ₹2,500 ($30).

The area lacks tourist attractions but provides quick access to both the airport and central Kolkata via the Airport Express metro line. The journey to Park Street takes 45 minutes by metro.

## Budget-Based Recommendations

**Ultra Budget (₹800-1,500/$10-18 per night)**: Stay on Sudder Street in guesthouses or dormitories. Modern Lodge and YMCA provide basic but clean accommodation.

**Mid-Budget (₹2,000-4,000/$24-48 per night)**: Park Street area offers the best value with central location. Hotel Hindusthan International and Hotel Galaxy provide good service at reasonable prices.

**Luxury (₹5,000+/$60+ per night)**: The Oberoi Grand on Jawaharlal Nehru Road epitomizes colonial elegance at ₹8,500 ($102). ITC Sonar near the airport offers modern luxury at ₹5,800 ($70).

## Transport Connections Matter

Consider metro connectivity when choosing your location. Park Street, Esplanade, and Salt Lake have direct metro access. Sudder Street requires a 10-minute walk to Park Street station. The Airport Express line connects the airport to central areas efficiently.

Taxis and app-based rides work throughout the city, but metro travel saves money and time. A metro day pass costs ₹45 ($0.54) and covers unlimited travel.

When [planning how many days to visit Kolkata](/kolkata/how-many-days-visit-kolkata/), your accommodation choice affects how much you can see efficiently. Central locations like Park Street maximize your time at attractions, while areas like Salt Lake offer comfort but require more travel time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Which area is best for first-time visitors to Kolkata?**

Park Street and Esplanade offer the best base for first-time visitors. You'll be within walking distance of major attractions like Victoria Memorial and Indian Museum, with easy metro access to other areas.

**Where do backpackers stay in Kolkata?**

Sudder Street is Kolkata's backpacker hub with budget hotels and guesthouses ranging from ₹800-2,000 ($10-24) per night. It's central, safe, and has plenty of budget restaurants and travel agencies.

**Is Salt Lake a good area to stay in Kolkata?**

Salt Lake offers modern infrastructure with shopping malls, restaurants, and newer hotels. It's quieter than central Kolkata but requires metro or taxi rides to reach major tourist attractions in the city center.
