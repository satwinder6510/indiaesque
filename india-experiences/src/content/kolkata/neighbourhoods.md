Kolkata sprawls across both banks of the Hooghly River, but most visitor activity concentrates in the eastern sections — from North Kolkata's heritage lanes to Park Street's colonial elegance to the modern developments of Salt Lake.

This guide breaks down Kolkata's key neighbourhoods. For accommodation details, see our [where to stay guide](/kolkata/where-to-stay-kolkata/).

---

## Central Kolkata

The colonial-era core — government buildings, major monuments, and the city's most recognizable landmarks.

### Park Street & Chowringhee

**The vibe:** Kolkata's most iconic stretch. Colonial-era buildings, storied restaurants, and cosmopolitan energy.

**Key sights:**
- Indian Museum
- St. Paul's Cathedral
- South Park Street Cemetery
- New Market

**Eating:**
- Flurys — Art Deco tearoom since 1927
- Peter Cat — famous for Chelo Kebab
- Mocambo — continental classics
- Blue & Beyond — rooftop dining

**Stay here if:** First visit, want central location, prefer colonial atmosphere and restaurants.

**Hotels:** The Park, The Oberoi Grand, various mid-range options nearby.

**Getting around:** Metro (Park Street station), taxis, walking within the area.

---

### Esplanade & BBD Bagh

**The vibe:** Government and commercial district. Grand colonial architecture, crowded markets, working Kolkata.

**Key sights:**
- Victoria Memorial
- Maidan (city's green lung)
- Writers' Building (under renovation)
- St. John's Church

**Key areas:**
- **BBD Bagh:** Historic squares, colonial offices
- **Esplanade:** Transport hub, shopping
- **Maidan:** The vast open ground at the city's heart

**Eating:**
- Street food vendors near Esplanade
- Tea stalls and roll shops
- KC Das for sweets

**Stay here if:** Rarely — limited quality hotels. Better to visit from Park Street base.

---

## North Kolkata

The historic heart. Narrow lanes, ancestral mansions, traditional markets, and Bengali culture at its most authentic.

### College Street & Presidency Area

**The vibe:** World's largest second-hand book market. Intellectuals, students, and coffee house adda.

**Key sights:**
- College Street book market
- Indian Coffee House
- Presidency University
- Sanskrit College

**Eating:**
- Indian Coffee House — the original
- Paramount — cold coffee and snacks
- Street food vendors

**Stay here if:** Intellectually curious, love books, don't mind basic accommodation.

**Getting around:** Walk College Street; tram to Esplanade.

---

### Kumartuli & Sovabazar

**The vibe:** Artisan quarter where Durga Puja idols are made. Heritage mansions, temples, and narrow lanes.

**Key sights:**
- Kumartuli idol-making workshops
- Marble Palace (by appointment)
- Sovabazar Rajbari (heritage mansion)
- Temples along the ghats

**Eating:**
- Traditional Bengali in local eateries
- Street food and sweets
- Balaram Mullick & Radharaman Mullick

**Stay here if:** Heritage focus, want authentic North Kolkata experience.

**Getting around:** Walking within; metro or taxi to central areas.

---

### Jorasanko & Chitpur

**The vibe:** Tagore family heritage area. Traditional crafts, historic trades, cultural roots.

**Key sights:**
- Jorasanko Thakurbari (Tagore's ancestral home)
- Chitpur Road — brass, musical instruments
- Old Chinese quarter remnants

**Eating:**
- Anadi Cabin — no-frills since 1924
- Paramount Sherbet
- Traditional sweet shops

---

## South Kolkata

Residential, leafy, and increasingly hip. Good restaurants, lakes, and a different pace.

### Ballygunge & Southern Avenue

**The vibe:** Upscale residential with excellent restaurants. Leafy streets, lakes, and cultural venues.

**Key sights:**
- Rabindra Sarobar Lake
- Birla Planetarium (nearby)
- Kalighat Temple

**Eating:**
- 6 Ballygunge Place — Bengali fine dining
- Bhojohori Manna — authentic Bengali
- Oh! Calcutta — upscale Bengali

**Stay here if:** Want quieter area, local restaurant scene, don't mind taxi to monuments.

---

### Alipore & National Library Area

**The vibe:** Old-money neighbourhood. Consulates, the zoo, and green spaces.

**Key sights:**
- Alipore Zoo (Zoological Garden)
- National Library
- Horticultural Gardens

**Stay here if:** Visiting the zoo with kids, prefer green surroundings.

---

## New Town & Salt Lake

**The vibe:** Planned townships east of the city. Modern infrastructure, malls, tech parks.

**Key areas:**

**Salt Lake (Bidhannagar):**
- Planned residential and commercial
- Central Park, City Centre mall
- Tech offices and modern restaurants

**Rajarhat/New Town:**
- Near the airport
- Eco Park
- Convention centres, modern hotels

**Stay here if:** Near airport, business travel, prefer modern facilities over character.

**Hotels:** Novotel, Hyatt Regency, various business hotels.

**Note:** Far from tourist sights. Only recommended for specific purposes.

---

## Howrah (West Bank)

**The vibe:** Industrial and transport hub across the river. Gritty, working-class, architecturally interesting.

**Key sights:**
- Howrah Bridge — iconic cantilever bridge
- Howrah Station — colonial railway terminus
- Belur Math — Ramakrishna Mission headquarters
- Dakshineswar Temple — historic Kali temple

**Stay here if:** Rarely — limited tourist infrastructure. Visit sights but stay in central Kolkata.

**Getting around:** Ferry across the Hooghly, or road via bridges.

## Kolkata Layout

The city runs roughly north-south along the Hooghly River:

```
HOWRAH ← [Howrah Bridge] → NORTH KOLKATA
                              ↓
                          ESPLANADE
                              ↓
                         PARK STREET
                              ↓
                         BALLYGUNGE
                              ↓
                          ALIPORE

            SALT LAKE (east) ←→ Central areas
```

**Travel times (non-rush hour):**
- Park Street to North Kolkata: 20-30 minutes
- Park Street to Howrah: 15-25 minutes
- Park Street to Salt Lake: 30-40 minutes
- Salt Lake to Airport: 20-30 minutes

## Getting Between Neighbourhoods

**Metro:** Clean, efficient, covers major tourist corridors. Park Street, Esplanade, Maidan stations.

**Trams:** Atmospheric but slow. Best for North Kolkata experience.

**Taxis/Uber/Ola:** Widely available. Yellow Ambassador taxis use meters; apps have fixed pricing.

**Ferries:** Cross the Hooghly from various ghats. Scenic and cheap (₹5-10).

**Walking:** Practical within neighbourhoods. Central Kolkata is walkable in cooler months.
