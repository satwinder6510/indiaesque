**Kolkata offers excellent value for budget travelers, with backpackers managing comfortably on ₹1,000-2,000 ($12-24) daily and mid-range travelers enjoying good comfort at ₹2,000-4,000 ($24-48) per day, including accommodation, food, and transport.**

## Daily Budget Breakdown

### Backpacker Budget: ₹1,000-2,000 ($12-24)
- **Accommodation**: ₹400-800 ($5-10) - Hostel dorms or basic guesthouses
- **Food**: ₹300-600 ($3.50-7) - Street food and local eateries
- **Transport**: ₹100-200 ($1.20-2.40) - Metro, buses, and shared taxis
- **Activities**: ₹200-400 ($2.40-5) - Free attractions and temple visits

### Mid-Range Budget: ₹2,000-4,000 ($24-48)
- **Accommodation**: ₹1,200-2,500 ($14-30) - Private rooms with AC
- **Food**: ₹600-1,000 ($7-12) - Mix of street food and restaurants
- **Transport**: ₹200-400 ($2.40-5) - Metro, Uber, and auto-rickshaws
- **Activities**: ₹200-600 ($2.40-7) - Museums, heritage sites, and entertainment

## Budget Accommodation Options

**Sudder Street Area** remains the backpacker hub. Backpacker Lodge offers dorm beds from ₹500 ($6) with decent facilities. Times Guest House provides clean rooms from ₹800 ($10) for doubles. Hotel Maria charges ₹1,200 ($14) for AC rooms.

**Park Street vicinity** has mid-range options like Hotel Lytton starting at ₹2,000 ($24). The area puts you within walking distance of restaurants and attractions, saving transport costs.

**New Market area** offers budget hotels like Hotel VIP International from ₹1,500 ($18). You'll be close to shopping and the metro line for easy access to [heritage sites](/kolkata/heritage-sites/).

## Food: Street vs Restaurants

### Street Food Paradise
Kolkata's street food scene offers incredible value. Phuchka costs ₹20-40 ($0.25-0.50) per plate. Kathi rolls range from ₹40-80 ($0.50-1). A full meal of rice, dal, and vegetables from roadside stalls costs ₹60-120 ($0.70-1.40).

**College Street** serves excellent food near Presidency University. Try the famous coffee at Indian Coffee House for ₹25 ($0.30). **New Market area** has numerous food stalls offering Bengali breakfast for ₹40-80 ($0.50-1).

### Restaurant Options
Local restaurants charge ₹150-300 ($1.80-3.50) for complete meals. **Park Street** restaurants cost ₹400-800 ($5-10) per person. Consider joining [food tours](/kolkata/food-tours/) to discover authentic spots that locals frequent.

## Public Transport Costs

**Kolkata Metro** offers the best value at ₹5-20 ($0.06-0.24) per journey. Buy a smart card for ₹50 ($0.60) deposit plus travel credit to avoid queues.

**Buses** cost ₹8-15 ($0.10-0.18) for most routes. The AC buses charge ₹15-25 ($0.18-0.30) and provide more comfort during hot weather.

**Taxis and Auto-rickshaws** follow meter rates starting at ₹25 ($0.30). Expect ₹100-200 ($1.20-2.40) for cross-city journeys. Uber and Ola provide competitive rates with upfront pricing.

For comprehensive transport information, check our [Getting Around Kolkata guide](/kolkata/kolkata-transport-guide/).

## Free Attractions and Activities

**Victoria Memorial** grounds are free to enter - you only pay ₹30 ($0.35) for the museum inside. The surrounding gardens provide perfect spots for morning walks.

**Howrah Bridge** walking is completely free and offers great photo opportunities. Visit early morning or evening for the best light and cooler temperatures.

**Kalighat Temple** charges no entry fee. The spiritual experience and local atmosphere make this a worthwhile free attraction.

**Maidan** serves as Kolkata's green lung. Join locals for evening walks, cricket matches, or simply relax under the trees.

**Kumartuli** artisan quarter allows free exploration. Watch craftsmen create clay idols and interact with artists working on traditional pieces.

## Budget Shopping Areas

**New Market** offers bargaining opportunities and reasonable prices. Start at half the quoted price and negotiate. The covered market protects you from weather while shopping.

**Gariahat Market** provides local shopping experiences at rock-bottom prices. Vegetables, fruits, and daily necessities cost significantly less than tourist areas.

**College Street** book market sells new and used books at fraction of original prices. Academic books, novels, and rare finds available at ₹50-200 ($0.60-2.40).

Our [markets guide](/kolkata/markets-shopping/) covers specific stalls and bargaining strategies for each location.

## Money-Saving Tips

**Eat where locals eat** - Follow office workers during lunch hours to find authentic, cheap restaurants. Avoid places with English-only menus near tourist spots.

**Use public transport** - Metro and buses save hundreds of rupees compared to private taxis. Plan your route using metro connectivity.

**Stay hydrated cheaply** - Buy large water bottles for ₹20-30 ($0.24-0.35) instead of small bottles for ₹10-15 each.

**Temple food** - Many temples serve free meals during festivals and prayer times. Kali Temple and ISKCON often provide free prasadam.

**Group travel** - Share auto-rickshaws and taxis with other travelers. Hostels often organize group trips to reduce per-person costs.

**ATM strategy** - Use bank ATMs instead of private operators to avoid extra charges. SBI and HDFC have extensive networks with reasonable fees for international cards.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much money do I need per day in Kolkata?**
Backpackers can manage on ₹1,000-2,000 ($12-24) daily, covering dorm beds, street food, and local transport. Mid-range travelers need ₹2,000-4,000 ($24-48) for private rooms, restaurant meals, and comfortable transport options.

**What are the cheapest places to stay in Kolkata?**
Budget hostels in Sudder Street offer dorm beds from ₹400-800 ($5-10). Backpacker Lodge, Times Guest House, and Hotel Maria provide clean accommodation. Budget hotels in Park Street area start from ₹1,200 ($14) for private rooms.

**Is street food safe and cheap in Kolkata?**
Street food is extremely affordable at ₹20-100 ($0.25-1.20) per item and generally safe if you choose busy stalls. Stick to hot, freshly prepared items and avoid raw vegetables or ice from uncertain sources.
