**Luxury travel in Kolkata centers on three premier 5-star hotels - The Oberoi Grand, ITC Sonar, and Taj Bengal - complemented by Michelin-worthy dining, private heritage tours, and exclusive cultural experiences starting from ₹30,000 ($360) per day.**

Kolkata's luxury scene balances colonial grandeur with contemporary sophistication. You'll find world-class hotels in prime locations, award-winning restaurants serving both Bengali haute cuisine and international fare, and bespoke experiences that showcase the city's rich cultural heritage. The best luxury properties cluster around Park Street, New Market, and Salt Lake, putting you within easy reach of [Kolkata's top attractions](//kolkata/).

## Premium 5-Star Hotels

### The Oberoi Grand
Located on Jawaharlal Nehru Road, The Oberoi Grand commands ₹25,000-35,000 ($300-420) per night for their Grand Rooms. This heritage property, built in 1938, features a stunning atrium lobby, the acclaimed Threesixtythree restaurant, and the Oberoi Spa. Book their Heritage Suite (₹65,000/$780) for 1,200 square feet of colonial elegance with butler service.

### ITC Sonar Bangla
ITC Sonar in Salt Lake charges ₹20,000-28,000 ($240-335) for Executive Rooms. Their Royal Bengal wing houses the Michelin Guide-mentioned Rajbhavan restaurant and the award-winning Kaya Kalp spa. The Presidential Suite (₹1,20,000/$1,440) spans 2,400 square feet with panoramic city views.

### Taj Bengal
Taj Bengal on Belvedere Road offers rooms from ₹22,000-30,000 ($265-360). Their Souk restaurant serves Mediterranean cuisine, while Chinoiserie specializes in Cantonese fare. The Palace Suite (₹80,000/$960) includes a private study and dining area.

## Fine Dining Experiences

### Michelin-Recognized Restaurants
Rajbhavan at ITC Sonar serves royal Bengali cuisine with tasting menus from ₹4,500 ($54) per person. Chef Sharad Dewan recreates recipes from erstwhile Bengali zamindars using heirloom ingredients.

Threesixtythree at The Oberoi Grand offers contemporary European cuisine from ₹3,800 ($45) for three courses. Their wine cellar stocks over 200 labels, including rare Burgundies and vintage Champagnes.

### Exclusive Private Dining
Book private dining at Bhojohori Manna's heritage location on Elgin Road for authentic Bengali thali experiences (₹2,500/$30 per person, minimum 8 guests). They arrange traditional folk performances with advance notice.

## Private Guided Tours

### Heritage Tours with Expert Historians
Calcutta Heritage Tours offers private historian-guided experiences for ₹8,000-12,000 ($95-145) per day. Dr. Jayanta Sengupta, former curator of Victoria Memorial, leads specialized colonial architecture tours covering Writers' Building, High Court, and St. Paul's Cathedral.

### Exclusive Cultural Access
Bengal Heritage Foundation arranges private access to closed sections of [heritage sites](//heritage-sites/) like Marble Palace and Tagore House for ₹15,000 ($180) per group. These tours include meetings with current family members and access to private art collections.

## Luxury Shopping

### Premium Shopping Destinations
Quest Mall in Ballygunge houses international luxury brands like Louis Vuitton, Gucci, and Jimmy Choo. Personal shopping services cost ₹5,000 ($60) for half-day assistance.

South City Mall offers luxury Indian designers and jewelry. For authentic experiences, book private shopping tours of [traditional markets](//markets-shopping/) with expert guides for ₹3,500 ($42) per day.

## Spa and Wellness Services

### Award-Winning Spas
Kaya Kalp at ITC Sonar offers signature treatments from ₹6,500-12,000 ($78-145). Their Royal Bengal treatment (₹15,000/$180) includes a 3-hour wellness journey with ancient Bengali healing techniques.

The Oberoi Spa features Ayurvedic treatments from ₹5,500-10,000 ($66-120). Book their couples' suite (₹25,000/$300) for private relaxation spaces.

## Premium Transportation

### Chauffeur Services
Premium car rentals through luxury hotels provide Toyota Camry or BMW 3 Series with experienced drivers for ₹4,500-6,500 ($54-78) per day. Mercedes S-Class options cost ₹8,500 ($102) daily.

### Helicopter Tours
Heritage Aviation operates scenic flights from Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose Airport. The 30-minute city circuit (₹15,000/$180 per person) covers Victoria Memorial, Howrah Bridge, and the Hooghly River. Book 48 hours in advance through luxury hotel concierges.

## Exclusive Cultural Programs

### Private Classical Performances
Surjit Singh Academy arranges private Kathak or classical music performances at heritage venues for ₹25,000-40,000 ($300-480) per evening. Performances at Tagore House or Marble Palace require 2-week advance booking.

### Bespoke Food Experiences
Join master chefs for private cooking classes at heritage homes in Jorasanko or Sovabazar for ₹8,500 ($102) per person. Learn traditional techniques like fish curry and mishti preparation in 200-year-old kitchens.

Planning luxury travel in Kolkata requires advance booking, especially during peak season (October-March). Most luxury hotels offer package deals including airport transfers, guided tours, and dining credits. For comprehensive planning, consider how long you need to [experience Kolkata properly](//how-many-days-visit-kolkata/).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What are the best luxury hotels in Kolkata?**
The Oberoi Grand (₹25,000/$300/night), ITC Sonar (₹20,000/$240/night), and Taj Bengal (₹22,000/$265/night) are Kolkata's top luxury properties, offering world-class service, premium locations, and exceptional amenities.

**How much does luxury travel in Kolkata cost per day?**
Luxury travel in Kolkata costs ₹30,000-50,000 ($360-600) per day including 5-star accommodation, fine dining, private tours, and premium experiences. This excludes international flights but covers all ground expenses.

**Are private helicopter tours available in Kolkata?**
Yes, helicopter tours operate from Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose Airport starting at ₹15,000 ($180) for 30 minutes. Book through Pawan Hans or Heritage Aviation for aerial views of the Hooghly River and city landmarks.
