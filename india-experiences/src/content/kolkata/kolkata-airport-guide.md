**Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport (CCU) sits 17km northeast of central Kolkata. You can reach the city center via prepaid taxi (₹300-400), metro connection, or ride-sharing apps, with the journey taking 45-90 minutes depending on your chosen transport.**

Kolkata's airport serves as the primary gateway to West Bengal and eastern India. The facility operates two terminals connected by a free shuttle service, handling both domestic and international flights efficiently.

## Airport Layout and Terminals

Terminal 1 handles all domestic flights with airlines like IndiGo, SpiceJet, and Air India. The terminal features check-in counters on the ground floor and departure gates on the first floor.

Terminal 2 manages international flights including connections to Bangkok, Dubai, Singapore, and London. This larger terminal offers more dining and shopping options compared to Terminal 1.

Both terminals provide free shuttle service every 10 minutes during peak hours. The ride between terminals takes about 5 minutes.

## Transport Options to City Center

### Prepaid Taxis
Book prepaid taxis at counters in the arrivals hall. Rates are fixed: ₹300-400 ($3.60-4.80) to central Kolkata areas like Park Street, Esplanade, or Salt Lake. Journey time ranges from 45-60 minutes depending on traffic.

The prepaid system eliminates haggling and ensures fair pricing. Keep your receipt and present it to the driver at the designated taxi stand outside.

### Metro Connection
The airport connects to Kolkata's metro system via bus shuttle. Board the airport shuttle bus (₹15) to New Garia metro station. From there, take the Blue Line toward Dakshineswar.

Total journey time to reach Park Street is approximately 90 minutes including the bus transfer. This option costs around ₹30 ($0.36) total but requires more time and transfers.

### Ride-Sharing Apps
Uber and Ola operate from designated pickup points outside both terminals. Costs range from ₹250-350 ($3-4.20) to central areas during regular hours. Surge pricing applies during peak times and bad weather.

### Private Cars and Rental Services
Car rental counters operate in both terminals. Major companies like Hertz, Avis, and local operators offer self-drive and chauffeur options. Daily rates start from ₹2,000 ($24) for economy cars.

## Airport Facilities and Services

### Currency Exchange and Banking
Multiple currency exchange counters operate 24/7 in both terminals. Rates are reasonable but slightly lower than city banks. ATMs from major Indian banks are available throughout the airport.

State Bank of India and HDFC Bank maintain full-service branches in Terminal 2 for complex banking needs.

### Dining Options
Terminal 1 offers basic cafes and snack counters including Cafe Coffee Day and local Bengali restaurants. Expect to pay ₹200-400 ($2.40-4.80) for meals.

Terminal 2 provides more variety with restaurants like Mainland China, Pizza Hut, and traditional Bengali eateries. International terminal dining costs ₹400-800 ($4.80-9.60) per meal.

### Shopping and Duty-Free
Both terminals feature duty-free shops selling liquor, perfumes, chocolates, and souvenirs. Terminal 2 offers a larger selection including traditional Bengali handicrafts and textiles.

Regular retail stores sell books, electronics, and travel accessories at standard Indian retail prices.

### WiFi and Connectivity
Free WiFi operates throughout both terminals. Register with your mobile number to receive login credentials. Connection speed is adequate for browsing and messaging.

Charging stations are available near all gate areas and seating zones.

## Nearby Hotels

### Airport Hotels
The Airport Hotel sits within walking distance of Terminal 1. Rooms cost ₹3,500-5,000 ($42-60) per night and include airport shuttle service.

Hotel Sonar Bangla operates near Terminal 2 with rates from ₹4,000-6,500 ($48-78). Both hotels offer hourly rooms for transit passengers.

### City Hotels
For longer stays, consider hotels in Salt Lake or central Kolkata. Check our [where to stay guide](/kolkata/where-to-stay-kolkata/) for detailed accommodation options across different budgets.

Many city hotels provide airport pickup services for ₹500-800 ($6-9.60) depending on distance.

## Parking Facilities

Short-term parking costs ₹40 ($0.48) for the first two hours, then ₹20 per additional hour. Long-term parking rates are ₹200 ($2.40) per day for covered spaces.

The multi-level parking structure serves both terminals with covered walkways. Valet parking services are available for ₹300 ($3.60) per day.

## Arrival and Departure Procedures

### International Arrivals
Complete immigration and customs procedures in Terminal 2. Duty-free allowances include 2 liters of alcohol and 200 cigarettes for adults. Declare amounts over $5,000 USD equivalent.

Currency exchange and prepaid taxi counters are located immediately after customs exit.

### Domestic Arrivals
Domestic passengers exit directly into the arrivals hall. Baggage claim operates efficiently with most bags appearing within 20-30 minutes.

### Departure Tips
Arrive 2 hours before domestic flights and 3 hours for international departures. Security checks are thorough, so pack electronics and liquids according to standard aviation rules.

Web check-in is available for most airlines, allowing you to proceed directly to security with mobile boarding passes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a taxi from Kolkata airport to the city cost?

Prepaid taxis from Kolkata airport to central areas cost ₹300-400 ($3.60-4.80). Book at the prepaid taxi counter in the arrivals hall to avoid haggling. Journey time is 45-60 minutes depending on traffic and destination.

### Is there metro connectivity to Kolkata airport?

Yes, Kolkata airport connects to the city via metro. Take the airport shuttle bus to New Garia metro station, then board the Blue Line. Total journey to Park Street takes about 90 minutes including transfers.

### What facilities are available at Kolkata airport?

Kolkata airport offers currency exchange, ATMs, restaurants, cafes, duty-free shopping, free WiFi, charging stations, and prayer rooms. Terminal 1 handles domestic flights while Terminal 2 serves international routes.
